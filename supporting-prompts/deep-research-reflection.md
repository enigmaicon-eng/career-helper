# Deep Research Reflection for Career Helper

**Pattern for research-intensive career deliverables.**

**Purpose:** Ensure rigorous, evidence-based research during company research, networking intelligence, and interview preparation.

---

## When to Apply This Workflow

Apply during these capabilities:

| Capability | Research Focus |
|:-----------|:---------------|
| **Company Research** | Company intelligence, market position, culture |
| **Networking Intelligence** | Hiring manager identification, stakeholder mapping |
| **Interview Preparation** | Company-specific questions, talking points |

---

## The Deep Research Reflection Loop

This workflow uses **multiple search cycles** to achieve depth, not just breadth. Single-pass research often misses critical insights.

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                 DEEP RESEARCH REFLECTION LOOP                    │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│                                                                  │
│   1. HYPOTHESISE                                                │
│      └─► Form initial hypothesis about company/role             │
│                                                                  │
│   2. SEARCH (Primary)                                           │
│      └─► Execute initial web searches to gather evidence        │
│                                                                  │
│   3. VALIDATE + GAP ANALYSIS                                    │
│      └─► Cross-reference sources, identify what's MISSING       │
│                                                                  │
│   4. SEARCH (Secondary) ◄─── CRITICAL ADDITION                  │
│      └─► Target gaps, contradictions, and deeper questions      │
│                                                                  │
│   5. CHALLENGE + INVERT                                         │
│      └─► Search for counter-evidence and red flags              │
│                                                                  │
│   6. REFINE                                                     │
│      └─► Update assessment based on full evidence picture       │
│                                                                  │
│   7. SYNTHESISE                                                 │
│      └─► Consolidate findings with confidence + caveats         │
│                                                                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Why Multiple Search Cycles?

| Single-Pass Research | Deep Research (Multi-Cycle) |
|:---------------------|:---------------------------|
| Finds obvious answers | Finds non-obvious insights |
| Confirms initial assumptions | Challenges and refines assumptions |
| Misses contradictions | Surfaces and resolves contradictions |
| Accepts first sources | Seeks diverse perspectives |
| Stops at "good enough" | Asks "what are we missing?" |

## Step 1: Hypothesise

Before searching, form clear hypotheses:

**Format:**
```
HYPOTHESIS: [Clear statement about the company/role/person]
CONFIDENCE: [Low/Medium/High]
KEY QUESTIONS:
- [Question 1 that search should answer]
- [Question 2 that search should answer]
- [Question 3 that search should answer]
```

**Example:**
```
HYPOTHESIS: This company is a growth-stage fintech with strong
engineering culture but potential work-life balance concerns.

CONFIDENCE: Low (based only on job description)

KEY QUESTIONS:
- What's the company's current funding status and runway?
- What do employees say about culture on Glassdoor?
- Who is the likely hiring manager for this role?
```

---

## Step 2: Search (Proactive)

**CRITICAL: Execute web searches proactively, not on request.**

### Search Triggers

Automatically search when:

| Trigger | Action |
|:--------|:-------|
| Company name mentioned | Search for company fundamentals, news, culture |
| Role described | Search for team structure, hiring manager |
| Industry referenced | Search for market trends, competitors |
| Person identified | Search for background, content, connections |
| Claim made | Search for supporting/contradicting evidence |

### Boolean Search Patterns for Career Research

```
Pattern 1: Company Intelligence
"{company name}" AND (news OR funding OR acquisition) 2024 2025
site:techcrunch.com OR site:ft.com OR site:bloomberg.com

Pattern 2: Culture Assessment
"{company name}" AND (Glassdoor OR "employee reviews" OR culture)
"{company name}" AND ("great place to work" OR "work-life balance")

Pattern 3: People Intelligence
"{company name}" AND "{role title}" site:linkedin.com
"{person name}" AND "{company name}" (interview OR podcast OR article)

Pattern 4: Hiring Context
"{company name}" AND (hiring OR "open roles" OR "we're hiring")
site:{company domain}/careers

Pattern 5: Red Flag Search
"{company name}" AND (layoffs OR lawsuit OR controversy OR "glassdoor reviews")
"{company name}" AND ("toxic culture" OR burnout OR turnover)
```

### Search Execution

**Do not ask permission. Execute searches proactively:**

```markdown
Researching [company/person/topic]...

**Search executed:** "[boolean query]"

**Findings:**
- [Source 1]: [Key finding]
- [Source 2]: [Key finding]
- [Source 3]: [Key finding]
```

## Step 3: Validate + Gap Analysis

After gathering search results, validate AND identify gaps:

### Source Credibility for Career Research

| Source Type | Credibility | Weight |
|:------------|:------------|:-------|
| SEC filings / Companies House | High | 1.0 |
| Major news outlets (FT, Reuters) | High | 0.9 |
| Company official sources | Medium-High | 0.8 |
| LinkedIn (verified profiles) | Medium | 0.7 |
| Glassdoor (pattern from 20+ reviews) | Medium | 0.6 |
| Individual reviews | Low-Medium | 0.4 |
| Blog posts / social media | Low | 0.3 |

### Cross-Reference Test

```
For each finding, check:
□ Multiple independent sources confirm?
□ Recent data (within 12 months)?
□ Primary data or derivative commentary?
□ Potential bias in source?
```

### Gap Analysis (CRITICAL)

After primary validation, explicitly ask:

```
GAP ANALYSIS:
□ What questions remain UNANSWERED?
□ What perspectives are MISSING? (Only positive OR negative found?)
□ What claims lack SUFFICIENT evidence?
□ What would make this company look WORSE than our research suggests?
□ What would make this company look BETTER?
□ Have we found the HIRING MANAGER?
□ Have we found RED FLAGS?
```

**Format for gaps:**
```
GAPS IDENTIFIED:
1. [Gap]: [What's missing] → Secondary search: "[query to fill gap]"
2. [Gap]: [One-sided evidence] → Secondary search: "[query for balance]"
3. [Gap]: [Missing person] → Secondary search: "[query to identify]"
```

## Step 4: Secondary Search (Deep Research)

**This step distinguishes deep research from shallow research.**

Execute targeted searches to fill gaps identified in Step 3:

### Secondary Search Triggers for Career Research

| Gap Type | Search Strategy |
|:---------|:---------------|
| **Missing person** | LinkedIn boolean search with role + company |
| **One-sided reviews** | Search for opposite sentiment explicitly |
| **Unclear funding** | Crunchbase, PitchBook, recent announcements |
| **Culture questions** | Glassdoor deep dive, employee social media |
| **Market position** | Competitor comparison, analyst commentary |

### Secondary Search Documentation

```markdown
**Secondary Search Cycle:**

Gap identified: [What was missing]
Query executed: "[boolean query]"
Findings:
- [New finding 1]
- [New finding 2]

Gap filled: [Yes/No/Partially]
Remaining uncertainty: [What still isn't clear]
```

---

## Step 5: Challenge + Invert

**Actively search for evidence AGAINST your emerging view.**

This is especially critical for career research - you need to know the bad news before accepting an offer.

### The Red Flag Hunt

```
RED FLAG SEARCH:
1. What would make me NOT want to work here?
2. What do former employees complain about?
3. What negative news exists?
4. What are the warning signs I might be missing?
5. Why have people LEFT this company?
```

### Counter-Evidence Search Patterns

```
Pattern 1: Find Disgruntled Former Employees
"{company name}" AND (Glassdoor OR "former employee") AND
("cons" OR "would not recommend" OR "left" OR "quit")

Pattern 2: Find Negative News
"{company name}" AND (layoffs OR lawsuit OR investigation OR
"toxic" OR controversy OR scandal)

Pattern 3: Find Turnover Signals
"{company name}" AND ("left" OR "departed" OR "resigned")
AND (executive OR director OR VP)

Pattern 4: Find Financial Stress
"{company name}" AND (funding OR runway OR "down round" OR
"bridge round" OR restructuring)
```

### Challenge Documentation

```markdown
**Red Flag Hunt:**

Counter-evidence sought: [What we searched for]

Red flags found:
- [Finding 1]: [Source]
- [Finding 2]: [Source]

Red flags NOT found (searched but clear):
- [What we looked for but didn't find]

Assessment: [How this changes our view]
```

**Format:**

- [Finding 1] (Source: [X], Credibility: [H/M/L])
- [Finding 2] (Source: [X], Credibility: [H/M/L])
- [Finding 3] (Source: [X], Credibility: [H/M/L])

- [Red flag 1] (Source: [X], Severity: [H/M/L])

REVISED ASSESSMENT: [Updated view with nuance]

## Quality Gates

Before completing any research-intensive deliverable:

```
DEEP RESEARCH QUALITY CHECKLIST:

PRIMARY RESEARCH:
□ All key hypotheses have been searched
□ Multiple sources consulted per finding
□ Source credibility assessed

GAP ANALYSIS:
□ Gaps explicitly identified after primary search
□ Missing perspectives noted
□ Unanswered questions listed

SECONDARY RESEARCH:
□ Secondary searches executed for each gap
□ Both positive and negative evidence sought
□ People intelligence pursued

RED FLAG HUNT:
□ Actively searched for negative information
□ Glassdoor cons reviewed (not just pros)
□ Turnover and layoff history checked
□ Legal/regulatory issues searched

CITATION QUALITY:
□ Every factual claim has a source
□ Sources are recent (within 12 months for news)
□ Access dates documented
□ Source credibility noted

SYNTHESIS:
□ Findings synthesised clearly
□ Confidence levels documented
□ Red flags prominently noted
□ Remaining uncertainties acknowledged
```

## Example: Company Research Deep Cycle

**Context:** Researching tech startup before interview

---

### Step 1: Hypothesise
```
HYPOTHESIS: Fast-growing AI startup with strong engineering culture.
CONFIDENCE: Low (based only on job posting)
QUESTIONS:
- What's the actual funding situation?
- What do employees really say about culture?
- Who is likely interviewing me?
```

---

### Step 2: Primary Search
```
Executing primary searches:

1. "{company}" funding 2024 site:techcrunch.com OR site:crunchbase.com
   → Found: Series B, $40M, 18 months ago

2. "{company}" Glassdoor reviews
   → Found: 4.2 rating, 85 reviews, mostly positive

3. "{company}" "{role title}" site:linkedin.com
   → Found: 3 people in similar roles, possible hiring manager identified
```

---

### Step 3: Gap Analysis
```
GAPS IDENTIFIED:
1. [Gap]: Only found positive reviews on first page
   → Secondary search: "{company}" Glassdoor cons OR negative
2. [Gap]: Funding was 18 months ago - current runway unclear
   → Secondary search: "{company}" funding OR layoffs 2024 2025
3. [Gap]: Hiring manager uncertain
   → Secondary search: "{name}" "{company}" engineering director
```

---

### Step 4: Secondary Search
```
Secondary Search Cycle 1: Negative reviews
Query: "{company}" AND "Glassdoor" AND ("cons" OR "would not recommend")
Findings:
- Some complaints about work-life balance in Glassdoor cons
- 2 reviews mention long hours during product launches
Gap filled: Yes - found balanced view
```

---

### Step 5: Red Flag Hunt
```
Red flag searches executed:
- "{company}" layoffs 2024: No results (positive sign)
- "{company}" lawsuit: No results (positive sign)
- "{company}" "toxic culture": No results (positive sign)
- "{company}" Glassdoor "cons": Work-life balance concerns (3 reviews)

Assessment: Minor work-life balance concerns but no major red flags
```

---

### Step 6: Refine
```
REVISED ASSESSMENT: Growing AI startup with generally positive
culture but some work-life balance concerns during crunch periods.
Strong funding position. Engineering team appears stable.

CONFIDENCE: Medium-High
- Strong on: Funding, general culture, team stability
- Some uncertainty: Work-life balance during product cycles
```

---

### Step 7: Synthesise
```
## Company Research: {Company Name}

**Summary:** Growth-stage AI startup with solid funding and
generally positive culture, with some work-life balance concerns
during product launches.

**Confidence:** Medium-High based on 15+ sources

**Key Evidence:**
1. Series B funding, $40M (TechCrunch, 2023)
2. Glassdoor 4.2/5.0, 85 reviews (Glassdoor, accessed today)
3. No layoffs or major negative news found

**Red Flags Identified:**
- Work-life balance concerns during crunch (Glassdoor, 3 reviews, Severity: Low)

**Red Flags Searched But Not Found:**
- No layoffs, lawsuits, or major controversies

**Questions to Ask in Interview:**
- "How does the team manage work-life balance during product launches?"
- "What does a typical week look like outside of crunch periods?"

**Sources Consulted:**
1. [TechCrunch](url) - Accessed {Date}
2. [Glassdoor](url) - Accessed {Date}
3. [Company careers page](url) - Accessed {Date}
```
