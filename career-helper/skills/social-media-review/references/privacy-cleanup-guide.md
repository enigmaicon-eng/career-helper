# Privacy & Cleanup Guide

**Purpose:** Practical, step-by-step guidance for locking down and cleaning up social media profiles before a job search. Focuses on what to make private, what to delete, and how to build a positive presence.

## The 4-Step Cleanup Framework

### Step 1: Google Yourself

Before touching any platform settings:

```
Actions:
1. Open a private/incognito browser window
2. Search for your full name (in quotes)
3. Search for your name + city
4. Search for your name + university/current employer
5. Search for your common usernames

Record:
- What appears on the first 2 pages?
- Any surprises?
- Any content you'd forgotten about?
- Any name confusion with other people?
```

**Teach the user to do this themselves** - it's a good habit to repeat periodically.

### Step 2: Lock Down Personal Accounts

Platform-by-platform privacy settings:

**Instagram:**
- Settings → Privacy → Account Privacy → Private Account: ON
- This hides all posts, stories, and followers from non-followers
- Review your followers list - remove anyone you don't know
- Check tagged photos: Settings → Privacy → Tags → manually approve tags
- Review story highlights - these can reveal a lot even on private accounts

**Facebook:**
- Settings → Privacy → Who can see your future posts: Friends
- Settings → Privacy → Limit past posts: Use "Limit Past Posts" to bulk-change old public posts to Friends
- Settings → Profile and Tagging → Who can post on your profile: Friends
- Settings → Profile and Tagging → Review posts you're tagged in: ON
- Privacy Checkup tool: facebook.com/privacy/checkup
- Check what your profile looks like publicly: View As → Public

**Twitter/X:**
- Settings → Privacy → Protect your tweets (makes account private)
- OR: Keep public but audit content (see Step 3)
- Review and remove old likes/retweets that no longer represent you
- Consider: for job searching, a professional Twitter can be an asset when public

**TikTok:**
- Settings → Privacy → Private Account: ON
- Settings → Privacy → Who can view your liked videos: Only me
- Settings → Privacy → Comment filters: ON

**LinkedIn:**
- Do NOT make private - this is your professional platform
- Use /linkedin-coach for optimisation instead
- Check: Settings → Visibility → Profile viewing options

**YouTube:**
- Review any uploaded videos - are they appropriate?
- Check comment history: youtube.com/feed/history/comment_history
- Consider unlisting or deleting old videos

**Reddit:**
- Consider whether your username is linked to your real name
- If so, review comment history for anything concerning
- Reddit comments can surface in Google searches

### Step 3: Content Audit & Cleanup

For platforms you want to keep public (or semi-public):

**The Quick Audit Method:**
```
For each platform:
1. Sort/scroll to your oldest posts
2. Scan for anything in the RED flag category
3. Delete or archive anything concerning
4. Work forward to present day
5. Pay special attention to: 2-5 years ago (university era for graduates)
```

**What to Delete:**
- Posts with offensive language or slurs (even if "joking")
- Content involving illegal activity
- Complaints about employers, colleagues, or clients
- Anything sexually explicit
- Aggressive arguments or confrontational threads
- Content that contradicts your CV or professional claims

**What to Archive/Hide (Not Delete):**
- Party photos that are just a bit much
- Very personal emotional posts
- Old opinions you no longer hold
- Content from a very different life stage

**What to Leave:**
- Photos with friends and family
- Holiday and travel content
- Hobby and interest posts
- Positive and constructive conversations
- Evidence of being a normal, well-rounded person

### Step 4: Build Positive Presence

After cleaning up, consider what you WANT employers to find:

**Quick Positive Actions:**
1. Update all profile bios to include your professional context
   - "Computer Science graduate | Interested in software engineering and data"
   - "Marketing professional | London"
2. Share or engage with industry-relevant content occasionally
3. Ensure your LinkedIn is optimised (use /linkedin-coach)
4. If you have a personal website/portfolio, make sure it's current
5. Consider pinning a professional tweet or post to the top of your profiles

**For Graduates:**
```
You don't need to become a thought leader overnight. Small things help:
- Add your degree subject to your Instagram bio
- Retweet something interesting from your industry
- Follow companies you want to work for
- Like posts from people in roles you aspire to
- Share a project you're proud of from university
```

## Managing Tagged Content

Content others post about you is harder to control:

```
Actions:
1. Enable tag review on all platforms that support it
2. Search for your name in photos/posts on each platform
3. Ask friends to remove any concerning tagged content
4. If someone refuses: untag yourself, or report the content
5. Set up Google Alerts for your name to catch future mentions
```

## Ongoing Maintenance

After the initial cleanup:

```
Monthly:
- Google yourself in incognito mode
- Review any new tagged content
- Check that privacy settings haven't changed (platforms update these)

Before each application:
- Quick scan of your most recent posts
- Check nothing new has appeared in Google results
- Ensure LinkedIn is up to date (/career-helper:status to check)
```
