# Preparation Checklist

Help the user gather the right materials before they start using career-helper skills. Better inputs produce dramatically better outputs.

## How to Use This Reference

1. Ask the user what they want to achieve (use AskUserQuestion)
2. Based on their answer, present the relevant checklist section(s)
3. Mark items as **Essential** or **Nice to Have**
4. Explain what happens if an item is missing

## Universal Essentials (Every User Needs These)

| Item | Why It Matters | How to Provide |
|:-----|:---------------|:---------------|
| **Current CV/resume** | Foundation for most skills - CV optimisation, interview prep, LinkedIn audit all build from this | Paste text, upload PDF, or provide file path |
| **Career goals** | Skills adapt recommendations to your direction - without goals, advice stays generic | Describe in conversation: target role, level, industry, region |
| **Target region** | Salary data, legal guidance, and cultural norms vary significantly | State your region: UK, US, EU, or APAC |

## By Scenario

### Applying for a Specific Role

| Item | Essential? | Why |
|:-----|:-----------|:----|
| Job description (full text) | **Essential** | ATS keyword extraction needs the complete JD, not a summary |
| Company name | **Essential** | Enables deep company research |
| Your current CV | **Essential** | Base document for ATS optimisation |
| Application deadline | Nice to Have | Helps prioritise workflow steps |
| Referral contact (if any) | Nice to Have | Shapes application strategy |

**What happens without the job description:** CV optimisation drops from targeted ATS rewriting to generic improvement. Keyword coverage analysis becomes impossible.

### Preparing for an Interview

| Item | Essential? | Why |
|:-----|:-----------|:----|
| Job description | **Essential** | Questions are generated from role requirements |
| Your CV | **Essential** | STAR answers reference your real experience |
| Company name | **Essential** | Company-specific questions and culture context |
| Interview stage (phone/technical/final) | **Essential** | Question types differ dramatically by stage |
| Interview format (panel/1:1/case study) | Nice to Have | Adjusts preparation approach |
| Names of interviewers | Nice to Have | Enables interviewer research |

**What happens without the interview stage:** You get generic preparation instead of stage-specific coaching. A phone screen needs completely different prep from a final-round panel.

### Improving LinkedIn

| Item | Essential? | Why |
|:-----|:-----------|:----|
| LinkedIn profile URL | **Essential** | Required for full profile audit |
| Career goals | **Essential** | Headlines and content strategy depend on your direction |
| Target audience | Nice to Have | Content strategy adapts to who you want to reach |
| Existing posts (if requesting content review) | Nice to Have | Needed for content review capability |

**What happens without the LinkedIn URL:** The profile audit cannot run. Screenshots or copy-paste of profile sections can work as a fallback.

### Planning a Job Search

| Item | Essential? | Why |
|:-----|:-----------|:----|
| Career stage (early/mid/experienced/late) | **Essential** | Advice adapts significantly by stage |
| Current situation (employed/between roles/redundancy) | **Essential** | Urgency and approach differ |
| Target direction (if known) | Nice to Have | More specific planning if you have a direction |
| Constraints (notice period, location, salary floor) | Nice to Have | Realistic planning needs real constraints |

### Career Transition (Fractional/Portfolio)

| Item | Essential? | Why |
|:-----|:-----------|:----|
| Current skills inventory | **Essential** | Portfolio design builds from your capabilities |
| Income goals | **Essential** | Rate setting and stream mapping need targets |
| Target region | **Essential** | Tax and legal structures vary dramatically |
| Risk tolerance | Nice to Have | Affects transition speed recommendations |

## Presentation Format

Present the checklist as a clean, copy-paste-ready list in conversation. Example:

```
Before we start, gather these materials:

Essential:
- [ ] Your current CV (paste text, upload PDF, or provide file path)
- [ ] The full job description (complete text, not a summary)
- [ ] Company name

Nice to Have:
- [ ] Application deadline
- [ ] Names of anyone you know at the company
```

Then ask: "Which of these do you have ready? We can start with what you have and fill gaps as we go."

## Key Principle

Never block the user from starting. If they are missing items, explain the impact and offer to proceed with what they have. Something tailored with partial inputs is better than nothing.
