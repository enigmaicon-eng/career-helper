## 2. STRENGTHS vs WEAKNESSES

### What's Working

- **Clear career trajectory**: Analyst (Wipro) -> Consultant (Accenture) -> PM (JPMC) shows deliberate upward progression
- **AI/ML positioning**: References to AI/ML teams at JPMC is a top-tier differentiator in 2026
- **Brand equity**: JPMC + Accenture are recognized globally - this opens doors
- **Domain breadth**: Cloud, data migration, fintech, AI - covers enterprise PM sweet spots
- **Consulting background**: Accenture experience signals client management and adaptability

### What's Broken (Priority Order)

#### Critical (Fix immediately - these get you rejected)

1. **No quantified outcomes anywhere**
   - Current: "enhancing customer experience and driving business growth"
   - Problem: Every PM on earth writes this. What customer experience metric improved? By how much?
   - Fix: Add specific numbers to every bullet

2. **Generic responsibility language**
   - Current: "Lead cross-functional teams to develop and implement innovative digital products"
   - Problem: This is a job description, not an achievement
   - Fix: "Led [X]-person team to ship [product], driving [X]% increase in [metric]"

3. **No product identity**
   - Current: "AI product outlook and strategy"
   - Problem: Which AI product? What does it do? For whom?
   - Fix: Name the product, the use case, and the user segment

#### High Priority (Fix before applying)

4. **Wipro section reads as Business Analyst, not PM**
   - "Production Validations", "process maps/flows", "lessons learned" - these are BA/QA activities
   - Reframe around product decisions, feature launches, and customer outcomes

5. **Passive voice and weak verbs**
   - "Understanding the product environment" - not an achievement
   - "Experience in working for cloud products" - this isn't even a bullet point, it's a statement
   - "Responsible for timely execution" - passive and bureaucratic

6. **Accenture first bullet is a run-on list**
   - Cramming the entire product lifecycle into one bullet dilutes everything
   - Split into specific achievements with outcomes

#### Medium Priority (Polish before interviews)

7. **Missing sections**: Professional Summary, Core Competencies, Education, Tools
8. **Inconsistent punctuation**: Some bullets have periods, others don't
9. **Tense inconsistency**: Current role should be present tense, past roles past tense

## 5. 12-WEEK CAREER PREPARATION PLAN

### Phase 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-2)

- [ ] Fill in ALL `[bracket]` placeholders with real metrics from your career
- [ ] Create a Master Facts document cataloging every quantifiable achievement
- [ ] Add Professional Summary, Core Competencies, Education, and Tools sections
- [ ] Update LinkedIn headline, summary, and experience to match new CV positioning
- [ ] Audit LinkedIn for recruiter visibility (open-to-work settings, skills endorsements)

### Phase 2: Product Sense & Technical Skills (Weeks 3-4)

- [ ] Read "Decode and Conquer" by Lewis Lin (PM interview bible)
- [ ] Practice 2 product design questions daily (Exponent, Product Alliance, or Stellar Peers)
- [ ] Brush up SQL if not already proficient (Mode Analytics free tutorial)
- [ ] Study A/B testing fundamentals and statistical significance
- [ ] Review 10 product teardowns of target companies' products

### Phase 3: System Design & Case Studies (Weeks 5-6)

- [ ] Study system design basics for PMs (APIs, databases, scalability)
- [ ] Build 2-3 anonymized product case studies from JPMC/Accenture
- [ ] Deep-dive into AI/ML PM content (this is your differentiator)
- [ ] Read target companies' engineering blogs and product announcements

### Phase 4: Behavioral & STAR Stories (Weeks 7-8)

- [ ] Prepare 15 STAR stories covering: leadership, conflict, failure, ambiguity, data-driven decisions, customer obsession, trade-offs, influence without authority
- [ ] Map stories to Amazon Leadership Principles and Google PM competencies
- [ ] Practice "Why PM?", "Why [company]?", "Tell me about yourself" narratives
- [ ] Do 2 mock behavioral interviews with a partner or coach

### Phase 5: Networking & Applications (Weeks 9-10)

- [ ] Apply to 5-10 target roles per week (tailored CV per company)
- [ ] Reach out to 3-5 PMs at target companies on LinkedIn weekly
- [ ] Attend Hyderabad PM meetups (Product School, local communities)
- [ ] Seek referrals - referral >> cold application at every company
- [ ] Prepare a 30-second elevator pitch for networking events

### Phase 6: Interview Sprint (Weeks 11-12)

- [ ] 2-3 mock interviews per week (product sense, estimation, behavioral)
- [ ] Refine STAR stories based on mock feedback
- [ ] Research each target company's product portfolio deeply before interviews
- [ ] Prepare 3-5 thoughtful questions for each interview stage
- [ ] Practice salary negotiation scenarios
